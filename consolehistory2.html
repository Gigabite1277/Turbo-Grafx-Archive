The PC-Engine was a collaboration of two Japanese companies, Hudson Soft who managed the console design and a branch of the globally recognised NEC corporation, this instance the Home Electronics branch of NEC. The console was marketed as a 16bit console at the time boasting and 8 bit CPU and a 16bit graphics processor. So in reality it was a semi 16bit console, making it better than what was arround at the time for a short while. The console was first introduced to the games console market in Japan 1987 and quickly became popular amongst both Japanese and the gaming international gaming community through rare unofficial imports.
